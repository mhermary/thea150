Vagus Nerve

The vagus nerve is one of the longest nerves in the autonomic nervous system. It is responsible for lots of our physiology including blood pressure, heart rate, sweating and speaking. It is a critical nerve that when calmed, can greatly increase one's public speaking skills.

Amygdala

The amygdala is a part of the brain primarily associated with emotional processes. It plays a large role in emotional learning and memory. It is especially active in the response of fear.

Exercises

We learned many different exercises to calm the vagus nerve. My personal favourite was the foot tapping and the ear pulling. I found they did the best job at resetting my body and mind back to a calm state.

Vulnerabilities

Vulnerabilities can help speakers connect to their audience. After all, we are all human, and showing our own vulnerabilities makes us relatable to our audience and improves our storytelling skills.
